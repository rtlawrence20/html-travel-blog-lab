Adventures in Fire Island

Recently, I paid a visit to Fire Island National Seashore—a land that seems completely out of place,
secretly located just beyond New York City. I felt as if time had warped and I’d been transported to the past.
Scarcely a whisper of urban cacophony, the island thrummed to a different beat—soft, measured, and uncluttered.
I walked alone along wind-swept beaches with the sound of the waves my only companion.
I walked through the Sunken Forest, where twisted holly trees loomed above me.
It was there, in the shadowy tunnels beneath the green canopy, that I did something I hadn't done in years:
breathed in the stillness and felt a sense of renewal.

Sentinel of both natural and cultural treasures, Fire Island proudly bears its cloak.
Sailors Haven and Watch Hill are not merely quaint attractions but historic sites.
The Fire Island Lighthouse is a monolith against the nimbus pocked skies,
and horizons appear to stretch well past the end of the world. Fire Island is not just a place to visit;
it's a place that changes the way you view the world around you.
